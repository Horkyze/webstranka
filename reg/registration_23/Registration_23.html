Question : Do you find the revolution in Egypt to be succesfull after Hosni Mubarak has been thrown down? What are the biggest risks when a military council is running the country?
Answer :Even if at first sight a revolution resulting in toppling down a dictator seems to be highly successful, its chances to success need to be measured in the respective country\'s evolution post-conflict. Putting in place a military council might seem a proper solution for the moment, but in the long run, such an entity has no proper tools to perform a successful post conflict reconstruction as far as infrastructure, education, health care, welfare are concerned. Moreover, such a military council, being a technical unit in its very essence, lacks the elements that would unite the people around it ( it is very hard for the military to put forward issues that would help the people recognize the values and traditions a government would spread) and would eventually and might unfortunately fall in the opposite side, of turning itself into a military dictatorship. Therefore, we strongly believe that at the moment it is premature to consider the Egypt revolution a complete success and we feel that the military council is a transitional phase towards the final stage of democracy and a veritable government representing all Egyptians
