Question : Are you able to find ideological differences between Facism and Nazism?
Answer :There are two main ideological differences between Fascism and Nazism: Firstly, the racial policy of National Socialism was originally not present in Italian Fascism. Fascists did not demand the purity of race but rather obedience to the authority of the state from their citizens. They were nationalists, though. The difference was that under their doctrine, one could make a conscious decision to assimilate and become a worthy citizen of the state. This view was only altered when Italy became more entwined with Germany and the change met with some criticism. Secondly, the economic systems under Fascism and Nazism differed considerably. While both regimes intervened strongly into the economy, Nazism never developed the policy of dividing the citizens into classes according to their profession which was one of the cornerstones of fascist regimes.
