Question : Do you find the revolution in Egypt to be succesfull after Hosni Mubarak has been thrown down? What are the biggest risks when a military council is running the country?
Answer :The topic at hand is a very controversial one. It is not possible to fully analyse all the aspects of the Egyptian revolution as not all points of view can be compared to the fullest extent. Even though the ex-president Hosni Mubarak was deemed as a totalitarian dictator, we must realise that he kept the situation in Egypt stable. We cannot trust the information media presents as the ultimate truth and so the will of the people in Egypt can vary extensively. Only time can fully establish the answers to all causes and consequences of the revolution. The answer to the second question is similarly complicated. In the history of humankind military juntas sometimes took absolute control of the country (e.g. Burma 1962-2011). Nonetheless if an election comes we must consider the risk of Muslim Brotherhood controlling Egypt which would affect the balance of power in the region as well.
